
# Roman Empire 

## Overview
The Roman monetary system was one of the most sophisticated of the ancient world, evolving over centuries to support a vast empire.

## Early Period
- **Aes Grave**: Heavy bronze coins used in early Rome
- **As**: Bronze unit, the basic denomination


## Imperial Period
- **Aureus**: Gold coin, expensive
- **Denarius**: Silver coin, it is used for payment of soldier 
- **Sestertius**: Bronze/orichalcum coin, widely used, it is hard to find beautiful one now.

## Later Developments
- **Caracalla's Reforms**: Introduced the antoninianus in 215 CE, officially valued at two denarii but containing less silver, accelerating debasement.
- **Constantine's Era**: Replaced the aureus with the solidus (gold coin), establishing a new monetary standard that would outlast the empire itself. Bronze coins are quite cheep still 2026.
